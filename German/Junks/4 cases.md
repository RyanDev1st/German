| Case                       | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural | Example Pronoun | Example Usage with "Mann" (man)                        |
| -------------------------- | --------- | -------- | ------ | ------ | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Nominative (Subject)       | der       | die      | das    | die    | er (he)         | Der Mann gibt ein Buch. (The man gives a book.)        |
| Accusative (Direct Object) | den       | die      | das    | die    | ihn (him)       | Ich sehe den Mann. (I see the man.)                    |
| Dative (Indirect Object)   | dem       | der      | dem    | den    | ihm (to him)    | Ich gebe dem Mann ein Buch. (I give the man a book.)   |
| Genitive (Possession)      | des       | der      | des    | der    | seiner (his)    | Das ist das Buch des Mannes. (That is the man's book.) |
**Explanation:**

- **Nominative:** Marks the subject of the sentence, the one performing the action.
- **Accusative:** Marks the direct object, the thing directly receiving the action of the verb.
- **Dative:** Marks the indirect object, the recipient or beneficiary of the action.
- **Genitive:** Shows possession or a close relationship between nouns.

**Important Notes:**

- **Articles:** The articles ("the", "a") change depending on the case _and_ the gender of the noun.
- **Pronouns:** Personal pronouns also change their form according to the case.
- **Adjectives:** Adjectives preceding nouns also change their endings based on the case, gender, and number of the noun.
